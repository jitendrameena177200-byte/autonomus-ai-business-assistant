Trident Group — Corporate Expense Policy
Effective Date: February 2026
Owner: Finance Department

1. Purpose
This policy defines what business expenses Trident Group will reimburse and
the process employees must follow to submit expense reports.

2. Reimbursable Expenses
- Client-facing travel (flights, ground transport, lodging) booked through
  the approved corporate travel portal.
- Meals during business travel, up to $75/day domestic and $100/day
  international, itemized receipts required for any single expense over $25.
- Client entertainment, up to $150 per client per event, requires
  pre-approval from a Director or above.
- Conference and training fees directly related to an employee's role,
  subject to manager approval.
- Home office equipment for approved remote employees, up to $500 per
  calendar year.

3. Non-Reimbursable Expenses
- First-class or business-class airfare for flights under 6 hours
  (exceptions require VP-level approval).
- Alcohol at internal (non-client) team events beyond $30 per person.
- Personal entertainment, mini-bar charges, and traffic/parking fines.
- Spousal or family travel costs.

4. Submission Process
All expense reports must be submitted through the Expensify platform within
30 days of the expense being incurred. Reports submitted after 60 days
require Finance Director approval and may be denied at Finance's discretion.
Each line item must include a receipt image; expenses under $25 may use a
digital record in lieu of a paper receipt.

5. Approval Chain
- Under $500: direct manager approval.
- $500-$5,000: Director approval required in addition to manager.
- Over $5,000: VP or CFO approval required.

6. Corporate Card Usage
Employees issued a corporate card must reconcile all charges monthly.
Repeated late reconciliation (more than 2 consecutive months) may result in
card suspension pending a review with the employee's manager and Finance.

7. Policy Violations
Misuse of company funds, including submitting personal expenses as business
expenses, is treated as a serious policy violation and may result in
disciplinary action up to and including termination, consistent with the
Employee Handbook's conduct standards.
