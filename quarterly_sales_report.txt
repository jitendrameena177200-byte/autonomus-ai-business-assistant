Trident Group — Q3 FY2026 Sales Performance Report
Prepared by: Sales Operations
Date: October 5, 2026

1. Overview
Trident Group closed Q3 FY2026 with total revenue of $18.4M, up 14.2% year-over-year
and 6.1% quarter-over-quarter. Growth was driven primarily by the Industrial
Equipment division and continued expansion in the Southeast Asia region.

2. Revenue by Division
- Industrial Equipment: $9.6M (up 21% YoY) - strongest performer, driven by the
  new TridentFlow pump line launched in July.
- Logistics & Supply Chain Services: $5.1M (up 8% YoY)
- Consumer Hardware: $2.7M (down 3% YoY) - softness attributed to delayed retail
  restocking after Q2 overstock correction.
- Other / Licensing: $1.0M (flat YoY)

3. Regional Breakdown
- North America: $8.2M (44.6% of total revenue)
- Europe: $4.9M (26.6%)
- Southeast Asia: $3.8M (20.7%), up 32% YoY - fastest-growing region, led by new
  distribution partnerships in Vietnam and Indonesia signed in May.
- Rest of World: $1.5M (8.1%)

4. Key Wins
- Signed a 3-year supply agreement with Meridian Manufacturing worth an estimated
  $4.2M over the contract term.
- Closed 212 new mid-market accounts through the revamped inside-sales program,
  a 38% increase over Q2.
- Reduced average sales cycle length from 47 days to 39 days after introducing
  the streamlined quoting tool in August.

5. Risks and Watch Items
- Consumer Hardware division has missed forecast for two consecutive quarters;
  leadership is reviewing whether to reposition the product line or sunset it
  by end of FY2026.
- Component lead times from two key suppliers remain elevated (10-12 weeks vs.
  historical 6-8 weeks), creating fulfillment risk for Q4 if demand continues
  to grow in Industrial Equipment.
- Currency headwinds in the Europe region reduced reported revenue by
  approximately $180K versus constant-currency terms.

6. Q4 Outlook
Sales Operations forecasts Q4 revenue of $19.8M-$20.5M, assuming Industrial
Equipment maintains its current growth trajectory and Southeast Asia
distribution ramps as planned. The team recommends prioritizing supplier
diversification to de-risk the Q4 and Q1 fulfillment pipeline.
